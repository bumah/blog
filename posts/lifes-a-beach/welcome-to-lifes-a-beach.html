I used to think a calm, easy life was something you either had or you didn't — a matter of temperament, or luck, or how much was landing on you in a given week. The older I get, the less I believe that. The people who seem to move through life lightly aren't just wired differently. They've quietly built systems — small, repeatable ways of thinking and deciding that take the friction out of everyday life.

This blog is where I write about the ones I'm adopting.

Why systems, not willpower

Willpower is a terrible long-term strategy. It's expensive, it runs out, and it fails you on exactly the days you need it most. A system is the opposite: you set it up once, and it keeps working whether or not you feel like it. The point isn't to be more disciplined — it's to need less discipline, because the good default is already baked in.

That's the thread running through everything I'll write here. Not life hacks, not productivity for its own sake, but the handful of mental models and simple routines that genuinely make a life easier, clearer, and more enjoyable to live.

What "Life's a Beach" means to me

The name's a bit of a wink, but I mean it seriously. A beach is calm, unhurried, and uncomplicated — and getting your life to feel that way is real work underneath the surface. The ease is the outcome. The systems are how you get there.

So the goal isn't to pretend everything's effortless. It's to do the quiet setup work once, so that day to day, things actually can be.

What's coming

Over time I'll share the specific frameworks I lean on — the little acronyms and rules I've built for myself to make decisions faster, worry less, and enjoy more. Some I've stolen and adapted; some I've made up. Each one gets its own post: what it is, why I use it, and where it's earned its keep.

If any of this resonates, stick around. I'm figuring it out in the open, one system at a time.
