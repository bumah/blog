Influence has a bad reputation, usually because people confuse it with manipulation. But influence is just the earned ability to move things — ideas, decisions, people — in a direction you believe in. Done honestly, it's one of the most useful things you can build.

The system I use has a deliberately memorable (and slightly cheeky) name: OPIUM — Observe, Position, Invest, Uplift, Mobilise.

Observe

Before you try to move anything, understand the room. Who actually decides? What do they care about? Where's the real energy, versus the org chart? Influence starts with paying closer attention than everyone else.

Position

Put yourself where you're useful and visible. Be known for something specific. People are influenced by those they associate with a clear strength, not by generalists who blend in.

Invest

Give before you ask. Help, connect, share credit — long before you ever need anything. Influence is a bank account, and most people try to withdraw before they've made a single deposit.

Uplift

Make other people better and more visible. Counterintuitively, the fastest way to grow your own standing is to reliably raise everyone around you. People rally to those who make them look good.

Mobilise

Finally, when it matters, ask — clearly and confidently. All the goodwill in the world does nothing if you never actually rally it toward a goal. Mobilising is where quiet influence turns into real movement.

Used cynically, any of this curdles into manipulation. Used to advance things worth advancing, it's simply how good ideas get the backing they deserve.
