I've been thinking about how I can take control of my longevity, instead of waiting for some expensive breakthrough to reach everyone. For me, longevity isn't just about living longer — it's about having a high quality of life for as long as possible. So I decided to treat it as something more familiar to me: an asset to be managed against two key risk factors, healthspan and wealthspan. How many years will you stay healthy? How many years will your money last?

ULTM8

ULTM8 is a free framework I built to help optimise both. It's built on eight pillars — four for health, four for wealth.

On the health side: a Healthy Heart, Lasting Endurance, a Strong Body, and a Healthy Lifestyle. On the wealth side: Debt Freedom, Financial Security, being Retirement Ready, and Income Independence. Together they cover the things that quietly decide whether your later decades are spent thriving or just getting by.

The goal isn't to overwhelm myself with complexity. It's the opposite. ULTM8 is there to help me improve my habits and track the indicators that matter, so I can take real control of my longevity — one pillar at a time, at whatever pace life allows. If it does that for someone else too, even better.

Why I kept it simple

I've worked in risk management for nearly twenty years, and if there's one thing that work teaches you, it's that a risk is best managed when it's well understood and consistently tracked. The same is true here. I wanted something that would work for me and could benefit others too — and for that, simplicity is everything. Make it too complicated and you end up overwhelmed, which defeats the whole purpose.

Where I'm at

I don't think of ULTM8 as a finished answer. It's a starting point — my own attempt to stop treating longevity as something that just happens to me, and start managing it like it matters.

It's free, and it's at ultm8life.com. If you're thinking about your own longevity the same way, you're welcome to use it too.

This is the start of the journey for me. More to come.
