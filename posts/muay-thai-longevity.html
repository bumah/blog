I've written about why I think Hyrox is such good longevity training. Muay Thai is the other side of that coin for me — and it earns its place for completely different reasons. If Hyrox is about building the engine, Muay Thai is about staying sharp, learning something, and actually blowing off steam while I do it.

I didn't take it up as a longevity thing. I took it up because hitting pads is ridiculously fun and I wanted to learn a real skill. But the more I do it, the more I realise it's quietly ticking a lot of the same boxes — just in a way that never once feels like exercise.

It's a brutal workout hiding inside a skill

Anyone who's done a few rounds knows Muay Thai is deceptively hard on the body. A proper session has your heart pounding for the Healthy Heart side of things, and the rounds just keep coming, which is about as honest a test of Lasting Endurance as you'll find. It's all core, hips, and full-body power too — throwing kicks and knees builds the kind of functional, connected strength that feeds straight into a Strong Body.

So on paper it hits three of my health pillars hard. But that's not really why I keep going back.

The part Hyrox can't give me

Here's what makes Muay Thai different: it's a skill. You're not just moving, you're learning — combinations, timing, footwork, reading another person. And that, it turns out, is brilliant for the bit of longevity we don't talk about enough: the brain.

Learning something complicated and physical at the same time — coordination, balance, reacting under a bit of pressure — keeps you sharp in a way that grinding out a run just doesn't. Coordination and balance are also some of the first things to quietly slip as we age, and Muay Thai trains both constantly. It's the most fun cognitive workout I've found, disguised as throwing kicks.

Then there's the release. There's something about a hard bag session that empties my head completely. Whatever I walked in stressed about is gone by the end. That's a direct hit to the Healthy Lifestyle pillar — stress is one of those slow-burn things that wears you down over decades, and I don't know a better reset button than this.

Why it stays in the rotation

So Muay Thai isn't in my routine because it's optimal on some spreadsheet. It's there because it's genuinely fun, it makes me feel sharp and capable, and it quietly looks after my heart, my strength, my head, and my stress all at once. The best longevity habits, I'm learning, are the ones you'd do anyway — and this is firmly one of those.

Hyrox keeps my engine strong. Muay Thai keeps me sharp and sane. Between them, I've got most of the health side covered by two things I actually look forward to — which is the only way any of this sticks.
